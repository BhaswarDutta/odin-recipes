Kadhi Chawal

Kadhi Chawal is a comforting North Indian dish made with tangy yogurt-based gram flour curry served over steamed rice. It's often tempered with spices like mustard seeds, curry leaves, and red chili, offering a perfect balance of flavor and warmth.

Ingredients

  • Yogurt (curd)
  • Besan (gram flour)
  • Water
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Salt
  • Ginger (optional, grated or paste)
  • Green chilies (slit or chopped)
  • Mustard seeds
  • Cumin seeds
  • Fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Asafoetida (hing)
  • Curry leaves
  • Dry red chilies
  • Oil or ghee
  • Cooked rice (for serving)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together yogurt, besan, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt with 2-3 cups of water until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into a deep pan and bring to a boil on medium heat, stirring continuously to avoid curdling.
  3. Lower the heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally until it thickens slightly.
  4. In a small pan, heat oil or ghee for tempering.
  5. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  6. Add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds (if using), a pinch of hing, curry leaves, dry red chilies, and green chilies.
  7. Saut√© for a few seconds until aromatic, then pour the tempering over the kadhi.
  8. Mix well and simmer for another 5 minutes to let the flavors combine.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice.
